PAPER I: STATEMENT & MATCHING MASTERY


TYPES OF STATEMENT-BASED QUESTIONS (NTA's FAVOURITE)


Type 1: True/False Statements

NTA gives two statements — identify which is true/false or both.


Example Pattern:

Statement I: Teaching is both an art and a science.

Statement II: Teaching is a purely mechanical process.

Options: (a) Both true (b) Both false (c) I true, II false (d) I false, II true


Tactic: Spot the absolute word (always, never, purely, only) → usually false


Type 2: Assertion-Reason

Statement I = Assertion (A)

Statement II = Reason (R)


Options Code:

(a) Both A&R true, R explains A

(b) Both A&R true, R does NOT explain A

(c) A true, R false

(d) A false, R true


Tactic (TRAP): Even if BOTH are true, check if R ACTUALLY explains A. If R is a correct statement but unrelated to A, answer is (b), not (a).


Type 3: Chronology / Sequence

"Arrange in correct order"

Tactic: Find the earliest known item first, latest last, then match.

PAPER I — STATEMENT & MATCHING MCQs


Q1 (Assertion-Reason):

Assertion (A): Formative evaluation is conducted during the teaching-learning process.

Reason (R): It provides continuous feedback for improvement.

(a) Both A&R true, R explains A

(b) Both A&R true, R does NOT explain A

(c) A true, R false

(d) A false, R true


Answer: (a)

*Reason: Formative IS during learning, and its purpose IS feedback. R directly explains A.*


Q2 (Assertion-Reason):

Assertion (A): Stratified sampling ensures representation of all subgroups.

Reason (R): In stratified sampling, the population is divided into strata and random samples are drawn from each.

(a) Both A&R true, R explains A

(b) Both A&R true, R does NOT explain A

(c) A true, R false

(d) A false, R true


Answer: (a)

*This is the textbook definition — R directly explains WHY A is true.*


Q3 (Match the Following):

List I (Concept) ↔ List II (Definition)

A. Reliability ↔ i. Measures what it claims

B. Validity ↔ ii. Consistency of results

C. Objectivity ↔ iii. Free from bias

D. Generalizability ↔ iv. Applicable to other contexts


Answer: A-ii, B-i, C-iii, D-iv


Q4 (Statement-based):

Statement I: In the deductive method, teaching moves from general rule to specific examples.

Statement II: In the inductive method, teaching moves from specific examples to general rule.

(a) Both true (b) Both false (c) I true, II false (d) I false, II true


Answer: (a) Both true

*They are the correct definitions of deductive and inductive methods.*

Statement-based Questions — Media Laws


Q1 (Assertion-Reason):

Assertion (A): Article 19(1)(a) guarantees freedom of speech and expression.

Reason (R): This right is absolute and cannot be restricted under any circumstances.

(a) Both A&R true, R explains A

(b) Both A&R true, R does NOT explain A

(c) A true, R false

(d) A false, R true


Answer: (c) A true, R false

*Freedom of speech IS guaranteed under 19(1)(a), but it is NOT absolute — reasonable restrictions apply under 19(2).*


Q2 (Statement-based):

Statement I: The Press Council of India is a statutory body.

Statement II: The Press Council can warn, admonish, or censure newspapers.

(a) Both true (b) Both false (c) I true, II false (d) I false, II true

Answer: (a) Both true

*PCI was established under the Press Council Act 1978 (statutory) and has quasi-judicial powers.*

B. Sedition ↔ ii. IPC Section 124A

C. Contempt of Court ↔ iii. Contempt of Court Act 1971

D. Official Secrets ↔ iv. Official Secrets Act 1923

Answer: A-i, B-ii, C-iii, D-iv

    SECTION E: STATEMENT & MATCHING MCQs — PAPER II


    Q1 (Assertion-Reason):

    Assertion (A): Agenda Setting theory suggests media tells us what to think about.

    Reason (R): Framing theory suggests media tells us how to think about issues.

    (a) Both true, R explains A

    (b) Both true, R does NOT explain A

    (c) A true, R false

    (d) A false, R true


    Answer: (b)

    *Both statements are correct but R does NOT explain A — they are TWO SEPARATE theories from different scholars.*


    Q2 (Statement-based):

    Statement I: The Shannon-Weaver model is a linear communication model.

    Statement II: The Osgood-Schramm model views communication as circular.

    (a) Both true (b) Both false (c) I true, II false (d) I false, II true

    Answer: (a)

    *Correct: S-W = linear (sender → receiver). O-S = circular/interactive.*
