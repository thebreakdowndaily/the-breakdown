Welcome Sequence

5-Email Welcome Series

When someone subscribes, they get 5 emails over 7 days. Each one builds trust, delivers value, and turns a subscriber into a loyal reader.

1

Welcome to The Breakdown

Sent immediately. Introduces the brand. Sets expectations. Delivers the first piece of value (link to best content). Clean, warm, no selling.

Subject: You're in. Here's what you get.

Sent: Immediately after signup
2

How We Think

Sent Day 2. Explains the methodology. "We don't do hot takes. We do forensic analysis." Shows the 5-step framework. Builds credibility.

Subject: How we analyze every story

Sent: Day 2
3

This Week's Best

Sent Day 4. Curates the 3 best breakdowns from the archive. Gives new subscribers immediate value. Shows them what they've been missing.

Subject: 3 breakdowns you should read

Sent: Day 4
4

The Stories We're Watching

Sent Day 6. Forward-looking. "Here's what's coming next week." Creates anticipation. Makes them feel like insiders.

Subject: What's coming next week

Sent: Day 6
5

One Thing You Can Do

Sent Day 7. Simple CTA. "Forward this email to one person who needs to hear this." Growth through word of mouth. No hard sell.

Subject: One thing you can do

Sent: Day 7

Platforms

Newsletter Platforms Compared

Choose based on your needs. All three support HTML emails, automations, and analytics.

Substack

Best for: Writers who want simplicity

Pros: Free until 10K subs. Built-in network. Discovery. Paid subscriptions.

Cons: Limited design. No HTML templates. Community tab can be noisy.

Price: Free (10% of paid subs)

Beehiiv

Best for: Growth-focused newsletters

Pros: HTML templates. Advanced automations. Referral program. Ad network.

Cons: learning curve. Free tier limited.

Price: Free (up to 2,500 subs) then /mo

ConvertKit

Best for: Creators who sell products

Pros: Visual automations. Landing pages. E-commerce integration.

Cons: Expensive at scale. HTML emails limited.

Price: Free (up to 1,000 subs) then /mo

Recommendation: Start with Beehiiv. It has the best balance of design, automation, and growth tools for a news analysis newsletter. The referral program alone is worth it.

Setup Guide

Email Setup Steps

1

Choose Platform & Sign Up

Create account on Beehiiv (recommended). Set up workspace. Connect domain if you have one.

  • Workspace name: The Breakdown
  • From name: The Breakdown
  • Reply-to: your email
2

Domain Authentication

Connect your domain for better deliverability. SPF, DKIM, DMARC records. This is critical - without it, emails land in spam.

  • Domain: yourdomain.com
  • Add DNS records provided by platform
  • Wait 24-48 hours for propagation
3

Design Templates

Create 3 email templates in the platform. Match The Breakdown brand. Dark header, white body, gold accents.

  • Newsletter template: Standard weekly edition
  • Breaking news template: For urgent stories
  • Welcome template: For new subscribers
4

Set Up Welcome Sequence

Build the 5-email welcome automation. Trigger on new subscriber. Space emails 1-2 days apart.

  • Email 1: Immediate (welcome)
  • Email 2: Day 2 (methodology)
  • Email 3: Day 4 (best content)
  • Email 4: Day 6 (upcoming)
  • Email 5: Day 7 (referral CTA)
5

Create Signup Forms

Build 3 forms for different placements. Embed on website. Add to social bios.

  • Website popup: Exit-intent, 5-second delay
  • Embedded form: Footer of website
  • Landing page: Standalone page for social promotion
6

Set Up Analytics

Connect Google Analytics. Set up conversion tracking. Monitor key metrics from day one.

  • Open rate target: 40%+
  • Click rate target: 5%+
  • Forward rate target: 2%+
  • Unsubscribe rate: <0.5%
