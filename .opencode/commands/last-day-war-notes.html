PAPER-I: TEACHING & RESEARCH APTITUDE

Unit 1: Teaching Aptitude

  • Levels: Memory (recall) -> Understanding (comprehension) -> Reflective (critical thinking)
  • Learner characteristics: Academic, Social, Emotional, Cognitive
  • Methods: Lecture, Demonstration, Discussion, Problem-solving, Case study, Project
  • ICT-based: SWAYAM (2017), SWAYAMPRABHA (32 DTH channels), MOOC, e-PG Pathshala
  • Evaluation: Formative (ongoing), Summative (end-term), Diagnostic (remedial), CBCS, Computer-based testing
  • Teaching Support: Traditional (chalk-talk) -> Modern (multimedia) -> ICT (online)

Unit 2: Research Aptitude

  • Steps: Problem -> Review -> Hypothesis -> Design -> Data Collection -> Analysis -> Report
  • Positivism: Objective, measurable, generalisable, value-free
  • Post-positivism: Critical realism, fallibilism, multiple methods
  • Referencing: APA (author-date), MLA (author-page), Chicago (notes/bib or author-date), Vancouver (numbered)
  • ICT in research: SPSS, NVivo (qualitative), Zotero/Mendeley, Turnitin (plagiarism)
  • Thesis writing: Chapterisation, abstract, lit review, methodology, findings, bibliography, appendices

Unit 4: Communication

  • Barriers: Semantic (language), Psychological (prejudice), Organisational (hierarchy), Physical (distance), Cultural (norms)
  • Effective: Verbal + Non-verbal; Kinesics (body), Proxemics (space), Chronemics (time), Paralinguistics (tone)
  • Classroom: Teacher immediacy, active listening, feedback, questioning
  • Mass media & society: Cultivation, Agenda Setting, Spiral of Silence
