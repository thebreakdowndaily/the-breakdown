## POST TYPES

### Type 1: Story Promotion
Promote a newly published story on thebreakdown.in.

**Visual**: Hero image from the story (documentary style) with gold "B" watermark + headline overlay in Bebas Neue.

**Caption structure**:
```
[Hook — 1 punchy sentence that states the core tension/paradox]

[2-3 sentences expanding the hook with the key numbers/facts]

[Bullet or emoji-separated breakdown of the story's key findings — 3-6 points]

[Bridge sentence connecting to The Breakdown's mission]

Call to action: Link in bio → thebreakdown.in/story/{slug}

[Hashtags — 8-12 relevant tags, no #follow #like spam]
```

### Type 2: Data-Driven Carousel (Swipeable)
Multi-slide post built around a single dataset or comparison.

**Slide 1 (Cover)**: Bold headline + "SWIPE →" in bottom corner. Gold on black.
**Slides 2-5**: One chart/data table per slide. Minimal text. Source citation at bottom.
**Slide N (Last)**: Key takeaway / bottom line + CTA.

**Caption**: Brief (2-3 sentences). The slides do the heavy lifting.

### Type 3: Weekly Roundup (Every Friday/Saturday)
Top 3-5 stories from the week. 

**Visual**: Card-based grid layout. Each card: category tag + headline in Bebas Neue.

**Caption structure**:
```
THE WEEK IN REVIEW — [Date range]

This week at The Breakdown:

1. [Headline] — [1-line summary]
2. [Headline] — [1-line summary]
3. [Headline] — [1-line summary]

[Closing line — what to watch next week]

Link in bio for full stories.

[Hashtags]
```

### Type 4: Explainer / Thread
A single concept explained in 4-7 slides (e.g., "What is the CPI?", "How the Strait of Hormuz affects your LPG cylinder", "5 charts that explain India's RTI backlog").

**Slide 1**: Question as headline (e.g., "Why does India's CPI score stay at 39?")
**Slides 2-N**: One idea per slide. Data visualisation or text-only.
**Last slide**: Bottom line + CTA.

**Caption**: Brief contextual hook + "Swipe for the full explainer →"

## CAPTION STYLE GUIDE

### Do
- Lead with a specific, surprising number or tension
- Use em-dashes and line breaks for scannability
- Keep paragraphs short (2-3 sentences max)
- Use **bold** for key numbers and terms
- End with a clear, low-friction call to action
- Include 8-12 relevant hashtags (not more)

### Don't
- No clickbait ("You won't believe...", "This is shocking")
- No exclamation marks in headlines (one per caption max)
- No emojis in headlines (use sparingly in body — 2-4 max per caption)
- No hashtag spam (#follow #like #instagram #viral)
- No breaking-news language ("BREAKING", "URGENT", "JUST IN")
- No political endorsements or partisan framing

### Voice Reference
- "India's anti-corruption watchdog collected **₹453** in penalties last year. The law allows up to ₹25,000 per case. There were 58,501 cases where information was denied."
- NOT "You won't believe how little India collects in corruption penalties!"
- NOT "BREAKING: MGNREGA replaced after 20 years!"

### Call to Action Options
- "Link in bio → thebreakdown.in/story/{slug}"
- "Full evidence-based analysis at thebreakdown.in (link in bio)"
- "Swipe for the full explainer →"
- "Follow @thebreakdowndaily for data-driven journalism."
- "Read the full investigation. Link in bio."

## PUBLISHING WORKFLOW

### Step 1 — Select Topic
- From trending intelligence (use `.opencode/commands/trending.md` if exists, or research fresh)
- Priority: stories published on thebreakdown.in this week
- Fallback: major news events that fit The Breakdown's editorial lens

### Step 2 — Research & Verify
- Pull key numbers, sources, and angles from the published story or research
- Fact-check all claims before publishing
- Every number must trace to a primary source

### Step 3 — Write Caption
- Follow caption structure for the chosen post type
- Run through the Style Guide checklist (Do/Don't)
- Count hashtags (8-12)
- Verify link works

### Step 4 — Specify Visual
- Describe the visual concept clearly enough for a designer to execute
- Include: aspect ratio, colours, typography, key text overlays, image sourcing

### Step 5 — Final Review
- [ ] Single, clear hook
- [ ] Key numbers are bolded
- [ ] No exclamation mark abuse
- [ ] No clickbait framing
- [ ] Factual, evidence-driven tone
- [ ] Link works (thebreakdown.in/story/{slug})
- [ ] Hashtags: 8-12, relevant, no spam
- [ ] Visual spec is complete and executable
- [ ] Brand colours and fonts used
- [ ] Watermark present (if applicable)
