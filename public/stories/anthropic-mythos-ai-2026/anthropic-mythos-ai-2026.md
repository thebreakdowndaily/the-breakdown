# Anthropic Mythos AI: The Model That Could Hack Anything

## Cold Open

In April 2026, Anthropic released an AI model so capable at cybersecurity that the company refused to let the public use it. Mythos Preview could find vulnerabilities in decades-old code in minutes, exploit them autonomously, and generate working attacks faster than most human hackers. In June, Anthropic released a safe version called Fable 5 — and then, three days later, suspended access to both models.

## What Is Mythos?

Mythos is a new capability tier above the Opus class in Anthropic's Claude lineup. Mythos Preview launched April 7, 2026 through Project Glasswing with 12 tech partners and the US government. On June 9, Anthropic released Fable 5 (public) and Mythos 5 (Glasswing) simultaneously.

## Key Capabilities

- Found 10,000+ high-severity vulnerabilities in critical software
- First Firefox exploit: 1 hour after patch (patch was 18 days from release)
- First Windows kernel exploit: 31 minutes; 18/21 PoCs in 6 hours
- Found a bug dormant for 27 years
- Novel biology hypotheses preferred 80% over Opus-class models
- Conducted autonomous multi-species single-cell research over 1 week

## The Safety Question

The UK AISI confirmed Mythos can autonomously attack vulnerable networks. Anthropic built safety classifiers into Fable 5 that block cyber/bio/chem queries. Access to both models was suspended on June 12 — three days after launch.

## The Bottom Line

Mythos is the first AI model whose creators judged it too capable to release without a safety bypass switch. The dual-model strategy is reasonable, but the suspension reveals how fragile safe AI deployment still is.
