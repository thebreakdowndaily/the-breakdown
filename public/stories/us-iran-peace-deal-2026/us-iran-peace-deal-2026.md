# US-Iran Peace Deal 2026: Full 14-Point Agreement Explained

## Cold Open

On June 17, President Donald Trump sat down at the Palace of Versailles with French President Emmanuel Macron and signed a two-page memorandum of understanding with Iran. The document ended the most expensive four months of American warfare since the Iraq invasion — and started a 60-day countdown to negotiate a permanent peace.

## The Deal

The Versailles Accord is a 14-point agreement that includes:
- Immediate ceasefire on all fronts, including Lebanon
- Reopening of the Strait of Hormuz (free passage for 60 days)
- US naval blockade lifted within 30 days
- Oil sanctions waived immediately for 60 days
- All sanctions to be terminated on an agreed schedule
- $300 billion reconstruction and development fund for Iran
- Iran reaffirms it will not develop nuclear weapons
- Enriched uranium to be down-blended on site under IAEA supervision

## What Each Side Got

**United States:** Ceasefire, Strait reopened, Iran no-nuke commitment, IAEA supervision. Did not get: end to Iran's missile program.

**Iran:** Sanctions relief upfront, blockade lifted, $300B fund, path to full sanctions termination. Did not get: immediate full sanctions removal.

## Key Sticking Points

Within days, disagreements emerged: IAEA inspections (Iran denies schedule), frozen assets ($12B disputed), enrichment timeline, EU sanctions remain in place.

## The Bottom Line

The Versailles Accord is not a peace deal. It is an agreement to try to reach one. Whether this becomes Trump's Camp David or his Munich depends entirely on the next 60 days.
