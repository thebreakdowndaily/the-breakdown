Cookie Policy

Last updated: June 2026

Our Approach

The Breakdown does not use cookies for tracking, advertising, or profiling. We believe journalism should not come with surveillance.

What We Actually Use

  • No cookies: We do not set any first-party or third-party cookies for analytics, advertising, or personalisation.
  • LocalStorage (optional): If you choose to follow a story, we store that preference in your browser's localStorage. This data never reaches our servers.
  • Plausible Analytics: Our privacy-respecting analytics provider uses no cookies and collects no personal data. See our Privacy Policy for details.

Third-Party Services

We embed no third-party tracking scripts. The only external services we use are:

  • Plausible Analytics (cookie-free, privacy-first)
  • Google Fonts (served via your browser's cache)
  • Email provider for newsletter delivery (your email only)

No Consent Banner

Because we do not use tracking cookies, we do not display a cookie consent banner. Under GDPR and similar regulations, consent is only required when cookies are used for non-essential purposes. Since we use none, there is nothing to consent to.

Changes

If this ever changes, we will notify subscribers and update this page. As of June 2026, The Breakdown remains cookie-free.
