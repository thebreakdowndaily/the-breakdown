Every Indian city has the same problems. Potholes that return every monsoon. Garbage that piles up unreachable. Footpaths that end abruptly. Manholes that swallow lives. Streetlights that stay dark. Water that arrives — or doesn't — on someone else's schedule.

These aren't mysteries. They're systems. And systems can be understood, redesigned, and fixed. The Fix investigates the machinery behind everyday failures — the contracts, budgets, laws, incentives, and institutions that produce them — and maps the evidence-based solutions that already work somewhere in the world.

Each story identifies a specific problem, traces its root cause through the system, documents who is responsible, and presents a concrete action plan: what citizens can do today, what government should do this year, and how other countries already solved it.
