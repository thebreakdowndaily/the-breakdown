Syllogisms — The 100% PYQ Topic


**Rules for "Only A are B" type:**

  • "All A are B" = A inside B circle
  • "Only A are B" = All B are A (reverse!)
  • "No A is B" = Separate circles
  • "Some A are B" = Overlapping circles

    • **Venn Diagram Method (3 statements):**

    Draw 3 overlapping circles → shade what's given → check what MUST be true


    **Opposition of Square (NTA LOVES THIS):**


    PairRelationTruth Value
    -----------------------------
    A (All S are P) ↔ E (No S is P)ContraryBoth cannot be true, both can be false
    A (All S are P) ↔ O (Some S are not P)ContradictoryOne true, other MUST be false
    E (No S is P) ↔ I (Some S are P)ContradictoryOne true, other MUST be false
    I (Some S are P) ↔ O (Some S are not P)Sub-contraryBoth can be true, both cannot be false

    **Memory Trick:** A-E = both can't be true / I-O = both can't be false / A-O & E-I = MUST disagree

    FallacyDefinitionExample
    ------------------------------

    CONFUSED CONCEPTS — PAPER II (NTA TRAPS)


    PairKey Difference
    ----------------------
    **Administrative vs Critical Research**Admin = serves media industry / Critical = questions the system (Lazarsfeld distinguished these)
    **Qualitative vs Quantitative**Words, meanings, small sample vs Numbers, stats, large sample
    **Content Analysis vs Textual Analysis**Systematic, quantifiable vs Interpretive, deep reading
    **Census vs Sample**Entire population vs Subset
    **Reliability vs Validity**Consistency vs Accuracy
    **Primary vs Secondary Research**Original data vs Using existing data
    **Nominal vs Ordinal vs Interval vs Ratio**Categories vs Order vs Equal intervals vs True zero
    **Mean vs Median vs Mode**Average vs Middle vs Most frequent
    **Descriptive vs Inferential Statistics**Summarizing data vs Drawing conclusions
    **Open-ended vs Closed-ended Qs**Free response vs Fixed options
    **Panel vs Trend Study**Same people over time vs Same questions, different people
    **Longitudinal vs Cross-sectional**Over time vs One point in time
    **Gatekeeping vs Agenda Setting**What enters news vs What's important
    **Circulation vs Readership**Copies sold vs People who read
    **TRP vs Reach**Viewership rating vs Number of viewers
    **Paid News vs Advertorial**Money for news coverage vs Advertisement disguised as news
    **Sub judice vs Contempt of Court**Under judicial consideration vs Violating court authority
    **Copyright vs Patent vs Trademark**Creative works vs Inventions vs Brand identity
    **Defamation vs Libel vs Slander**Civil wrong vs Written vs Spoken
    **Press Council vs News Broadcasters Association**Print complaints vs Broadcast complaints
