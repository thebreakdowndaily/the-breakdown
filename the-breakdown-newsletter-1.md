## STORY 1: THE US-IRAN PEACE DEAL

### The Headline
The US and Iran signed a peace deal. Hormuz reopens in 7 days. Oil drops 8.4%. The world just changed.

### What Happened
On June 16, US Secretary of State Marco Rubio and Iranian Foreign Minister Seyed Abbas Araghchi signed the "Agreement on Mutual Peace and Security" at the Grand Hotel Victoria in Evian-les-Bains, France. Fifteen months of indirect talks. Twenty-one days of direct negotiations. One signature.

### The Core Terms

| Term | US Gets | Iran Gets |
|------|---------|-----------|
| Nuclear | Full dismantlement | Civilian nuclear program only |
| Sanctions | Immediate relief | Frozen assets released |
| Trade | Oil market access | US investment |
| Hormuz | Reopens in 7 days | Fishing rights restored |
| Enforcement | Snapback if violated | Due process for violations |

### What Most People Miss
This deal isn't about Iran. It's about China.

China bought 1.8 million barrels of Iranian oil per day — 70% of Iran's exports — at a 30% discount. With Hormuz reopening and sanctions lifting, that discount disappears. China's energy costs go up. The US strategic position in Asia goes up.

Trump didn't just make peace with Iran. He undercut China's energy advantage.

### What to Watch
- **7 days:** Hormuz reopens. Oil shipments resume.
- **90 days:** Full nuclear dismantlement verified.
- **2026:** First US investment projects in Iran.
- **India:** Lower oil prices benefit the economy, but weaken India's argument for buying Russian oil.

### The Breakdown Take
This deal is real. It's significant. And it changes the map. But the real test is enforcement. Iran has violated agreements before. The snapback mechanism is strong — but only if the US enforces it.

**Confidence: 75% that the deal holds through 2026.**

## STORY 2: MODI-TRUMP AT THE G7

### The Headline
Modi and Trump met for the first time in 16 months. The hug was missing. The deadline moved to July 24.

### What Happened
PM Narendra Modi and President Donald Trump met at the G7 summit in Evian on Tuesday. Their last bilateral was February 2025 at the White House. Since then: 50% tariffs, three dead Indian sailors, and zero apology.

### The Five Issues

1. **Trade Deal:** 99% done. Last 1% = dairy, medical devices, agriculture.
2. **Dead Sailors:** 3 Indians killed in US tanker strikes on June 10. India summoned US charge d'affaires.
3. **Tariffs:** 25% base + potential 12.5% Section 301. Deadline: July 24.
4. **H-1B Visas:** Tighter restrictions signaled for Indian tech workers.
5. **US-Iran Deal:** Weakens India's Russian oil argument.

### What Most People Miss
Modi signed an EU deal, deepened France ties, thawed relations with China, and met UK/Canada/UAE leaders at G7. This isn't anti-American. It's strategic autonomy. India is building options.

### The Real Deadline
- **June 22-24:** USTR Greer in Delhi for final deal touches.
- **July 7:** Section 301 hearings on forced labour tariffs.
- **July 24:** Universal tariff expires. MFN tariffs apply if no deal.
- **Mid-July:** Target for interim framework signing.

### The Breakdown Take
The hug is gone. The hard bargaining has begun. Both sides have leverage. Both sides have constraints. The next 30 days determine whether this is a partnership that handles disagreement — or one that drifts apart.

## STORY 3: TELEGRAM BAN IN INDIA

### The Headline
India banned Telegram for 3 days. Durov is under house arrest. The paper leak is the justification, not the reason.

### What Happened
On June 16, India's Ministry of Electronics and Information Technology (MeitY) announced a three-day ban on Telegram under Section 69A of the IT Act. Pavel Durov, Telegram's CEO, was placed under house arrest at a five-star hotel. The stated reason: a fake AI-generated IIT-JEE paper leaked on Telegram.

### The Timeline
- **June 6:** Fake AI paper surfaces on Telegram
- **June 10:** IT Ministry summons Telegram
- **June 12:** Government says "satisfactory response"
- **June 14:** Telegram removes channels, calls it "premature"
- **June 16:** 3-day ban announced

Thanked on June 14. Banned on June 16.

### What Most People Miss
Section 69A doesn't cover exam fraud. It covers national security, public order, and incitement to offence. The government had to frame a paper leak as a "public order" issue to use this law. That's the real story.

### The Numbers
- 250 million Telegram users in India
- More than Russia, Iran, and Brazil combined
- Largest single platform ban in history by user count (if permanent)

### What Happens Thursday
- **Ban:** 250M users lose access. Government sets precedent.
- **No ban:** Government loses face. Durov walks free.
- **Compromise:** Durov's team agrees to new terms. Most likely outcome.

### The Breakdown Take
This isn't about a paper leak. It's about setting a precedent. The government can detain a foreign CEO, ban a platform in 48 hours, and cite a paper leak to do it. Every other tech company is watching.

## STORY 4: META AI MODE DEFAULT

### The Headline
Meta made AI Mode the default for 2 billion users. No opt-out. ChatGPT-style search is now the internet.

### What Happened
Meta rolled out AI Mode as the default experience for all Meta AI users across WhatsApp, Instagram, Facebook, and Messenger. 2 billion users. No prompt to enable. No opt-out button. If you have Meta AI, you now have AI Mode.

### What Most People Miss
OpenAI has 800 million weekly users. Meta has 2 billion. Overnight, Meta became the largest AI search platform in the world — without asking anyone.

### The Privacy Problem
Every search you type goes to Meta's cloud. What you ask. What you want. What you worry about. What you're planning. All stored. All monetized.

Google at least lets you turn off AI Mode. Meta doesn't.

### What to Watch
- **Regulators:** EU (GDPR), India (IT Act), US (FTC) — all have grounds to act.
- **Advertisers:** CPMs will drop as AI answers reduce clicks.
- **Users:** Behavior shifts from clicking links to asking questions.

### The Breakdown Take
This is the biggest AI deployment in history. Two billion people now have AI search as their default. The privacy implications are staggering. The advertising disruption is real. And nobody was asked.
