# PAPER I: STATEMENT & MATCHING MASTERY

## TYPES OF STATEMENT-BASED QUESTIONS (NTA's FAVOURITE)

### Type 1: True/False Statements
NTA gives two statements — identify which is true/false or both.

**Example Pattern:**
Statement I: Teaching is both an art and a science.
Statement II: Teaching is a purely mechanical process.
Options: (a) Both true (b) Both false (c) I true, II false (d) I false, II true

**Tactic:** Spot the absolute word (always, never, purely, only) → usually false

### Type 2: Assertion-Reason
Statement I = Assertion (A)
Statement II = Reason (R)

**Options Code:**
(a) Both A&R true, R explains A
(b) Both A&R true, R does NOT explain A
(c) A true, R false
(d) A false, R true

**Tactic (TRAP):** Even if BOTH are true, check if R ACTUALLY explains A. If R is a correct statement but unrelated to A, answer is (b), not (a).

### Type 3: Chronology / Sequence
"Arrange in correct order"
**Tactic:** Find the earliest known item first, latest last, then match.

## PAPER I — STATEMENT & MATCHING MCQs

**Q1 (Assertion-Reason):**
Assertion (A): Formative evaluation is conducted during the teaching-learning process.
Reason (R): It provides continuous feedback for improvement.
(a) Both A&R true, R explains A
(b) Both A&R true, R does NOT explain A
(c) A true, R false
(d) A false, R true

**Answer:** (a)
*Reason: Formative IS during learning, and its purpose IS feedback. R directly explains A.*

**Q2 (Assertion-Reason):**
Assertion (A): Stratified sampling ensures representation of all subgroups.
Reason (R): In stratified sampling, the population is divided into strata and random samples are drawn from each.
(a) Both A&R true, R explains A
(b) Both A&R true, R does NOT explain A
(c) A true, R false
(d) A false, R true

**Answer:** (a)
*This is the textbook definition — R directly explains WHY A is true.*

**Q3 (Match the Following):**

B. Validity ↔ ii. Consistency of results
C. Objectivity ↔ iii. Free from bias
D. Generalizability ↔ iv. Applicable to other contexts

**Answer:** A-ii, B-i, C-iii, D-iv

**Q4 (Statement-based):**
Statement I: In the deductive method, teaching moves from general rule to specific examples.
Statement II: In the inductive method, teaching moves from specific examples to general rule.
(a) Both true (b) Both false (c) I true, II false (d) I false, II true

**Answer:** (a) Both true
*They are the correct definitions of deductive and inductive methods.*

### Statement-based Questions — Media Laws

**Q1 (Assertion-Reason):**
Assertion (A): Article 19(1)(a) guarantees freedom of speech and expression.
Reason (R): This right is absolute and cannot be restricted under any circumstances.
(a) Both A&R true, R explains A
(b) Both A&R true, R does NOT explain A
(c) A true, R false
(d) A false, R true

**Answer:** (c) A true, R false
*Freedom of speech IS guaranteed under 19(1)(a), but it is NOT absolute — reasonable restrictions apply under 19(2).*

**Q2 (Statement-based):**

**Answer:** (a) Both true
*PCI was established under the Press Council Act 1978 (statutory) and has quasi-judicial powers.*

## SECTION D: COMMUNICATION THEORIES — STATEMENT DEEP-DIVE

### Normative Theories of the Press (PYQ Super-Frequent)

**The Four Theories (Siebert, Peterson, Schramm — 1956)**

| Theory | Control | Purpose | Example |
|--------|---------|---------|---------|
| Authoritarian | Government | State control | North Korea |
| Libertarian | Free market | Truth-seeking | Early US press |
| Social Responsibility | Self-regulation + public | Ethical service | Modern US/UK |
| Soviet-Communist | Party-controlled | Propaganda | USSR (historic) |

**Added later:**
- **Development Communication Theory:** Media serves national development goals (used in India, many Asian/African nations)
- **Democratic Participant Theory:** Media should serve diverse local communities

**Statement Q:**
"Which normative theory emphasizes that media has freedom but ALSO has an obligation to serve society?"
→ **Social Responsibility Theory**

### Key Theory Comparisons (Statement Q Goldmine)

**Agenda Setting vs Framing**
| Agenda Setting | Framing |
|----------------|---------|
| WHAT to think about | HOW to think about it |
| Issue salience | Issue interpretation |
| McCombs & Shaw | Entman (1993) refined |
| "The media told us COVID was important" | "The media framed COVID as a crisis vs a hoax" |

**Cultivation vs Agenda Setting**
| Cultivation | Agenda Setting |
|-------------|----------------|
| LONG-TERM effect | Short-to-medium |
| Shapes world perception | Shapes issue importance |
| Gerbner | McCombs & Shaw |
| Heavy viewers = mean world | What's covered = what's important |

**Spiral of Silence vs Public Sphere**
| Spiral of Silence | Public Sphere |
|-------------------|---------------|
| Individuals SUPPRESS opinions | Citizens DEBATE opinions |
| Fear of isolation | Rational-critical discourse |
| Noelle-Neumann | Habermas |
| Minority views go silent | Ideal democratic space |

**Two-Step Flow vs Hypodermic Needle**
| Two-Step Flow | Hypodermic Needle |
|---------------|-------------------|
| Limited effects | Direct effects |
| Opinion leaders matter | Passive audience |
| Lazarsfeld | Early media theorists |
| Media → Leaders → Public | Media directly → Public |

## SECTION E: STATEMENT & MATCHING MCQs — PAPER II

**Q1 (Assertion-Reason):**
Assertion (A): Agenda Setting theory suggests media tells us what to think about.
Reason (R): Framing theory suggests media tells us how to think about issues.
(a) Both true, R explains A
(b) Both true, R does NOT explain A
(c) A true, R false
(d) A false, R true

**Answer:** (b)
*Both statements are correct but R does NOT explain A — they are TWO SEPARATE theories from different scholars.*

**Q2 (Statement-based):**
Statement I: The Shannon-Weaver model is a linear communication model.
Statement II: The Osgood-Schramm model views communication as circular.
(a) Both true (b) Both false (c) I true, II false (d) I false, II true
**Answer:** (a)
*Correct: S-W = linear (sender → receiver). O-S = circular/interactive.*

**Q3 (Match the Following — SCHOLARS):**
A. Wilbur Schramm ↔ i. The Spiral of Silence
B. E. Noelle-Neumann ↔ ii. Mass Media and National Development
C. George Gerbner ↔ iii. The People's Choice
D. Paul Lazarsfeld ↔ iv. Living with Television

**Answer:** A-ii, B-i, C-iv, D-iii

**Q4 (Match the Following — BOOKS):**

B. The Passing of Traditional Society ↔ ii. Daniel Lerner
C. Pedagogy of the Oppressed ↔ iii. Paulo Freire
D. Manufacturing Consent ↔ iv. Noam Chomsky

**Answer:** A-i, B-ii, C-iii, D-iv

**Q5 (Assertion-Reason):**
Assertion (A): The Right to Information Act was enacted in 2005.
Reason (R): It replaced the Official Secrets Act of 1923.
(a) Both true, R explains A
(b) Both true, R does NOT explain A
(c) A true, R false
(d) A false, R true

**Answer:** (c)
*RTI Act is 2005 (TRUE), but it did NOT replace the Official Secrets Act — both exist. OSA 1923 still governs government secrecy.*

**Q6 (Statement-based):**
Statement I: BARC measures television viewership in India.
Statement II: ABC audits print circulation in India.
(a) Both true (b) Both false (c) I true, II false (d) I false, II true
**Answer:** (a)
*BARC = Broadcast Audience Research Council (TV TRP). ABC = Audit Bureau of Circulations (print).*

**Answer:** A-iii, B-i, C-ii, D-iv

**Q9 (Sequence — Chronology):**
Arrange in correct chronological order:
1. RTI Act
2. IT Act
3. Press Council Act
4. Working Journalists Act

Options: (a) 4,3,2,1 (b) 3,4,2,1 (c) 4,2,3,1 (d) 1,2,3,4

**Answer:** (a) 4(WJA 1955) → 3(PCA 1978) → 2(ITA 2000) → 1(RTI 2005)

**Q10 (Statement-based):**
Which of the following is NOT a non-probability sampling technique?
(a) Snowball (b) Quota (c) Stratified (d) Purposive
**Answer:** (c) Stratified — it IS a probability sampling method.
