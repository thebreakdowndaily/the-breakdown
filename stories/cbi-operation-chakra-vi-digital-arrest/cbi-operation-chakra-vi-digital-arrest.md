## Cold Open

On June 25, 2026, the Central Bureau of Investigation raided over 80 locations across 16 states simultaneously, deploying 60 special teams to dismantle a digital arrest scam network linked to more than 200 cases. Operation Chakra-VI — the sixth and most expansive phase of the CBI's cybercrime crackdown — resulted in the arrest of two key operatives and exposed a sophisticated modus operandi that included a fake website mimicking the Supreme Court of India's official domain.

The operation spanned states from Tamil Nadu to West Bengal. The CBI arrested B. Naresh from Chennai and Sanjib Saha from Kolkata, both accused of operating shell companies and maintaining a network of mule bank accounts used to launder approximately ₹2 crore in scam proceeds. Investigators seized digital devices, incriminating documents, and evidence linking the accused to a larger syndicate that has defrauded victims across multiple jurisdictions.

## The Fake Supreme Court Website

The scammers registered a domain deceptively similar to the Supreme Court's official URL and populated it with forged court orders, fabricated arrest warrants, and fake case details designed to convince victims they were facing genuine legal proceedings. The CBI's investigation was triggered by a formal complaint from the Supreme Court Registry, which detected the fraudulent domain and alerted law enforcement.

The fake site replicated the Supreme Court's visual identity, included fabricated cause lists, and featured counterfeit judicial officer profiles. It was designed to survive scrutiny by victims who might verify their "case" by visiting the URL they were directed to.

## Modus Operandi

The scam followed a carefully scripted sequence. Victims received calls or video calls from individuals impersonating police officers, CBI officials, or judicial officers. The caller alleged involvement in a serious crime — money laundering, drug trafficking, or terrorism — and claimed a warrant had been issued for their arrest.

To lend credibility, the fraudsters directed victims to the fake Supreme Court website, where fabricated charges and forged warrants could be viewed. The victim was then placed under "digital arrest" — confined to their home and required to remain on a video call for hours or days — while being coerced into transferring money to designated accounts to secure bail or avoid prosecution. Victims were isolated, threatened, and prevented from contacting family members or legal counsel.

## International Dimension

Evidence gathered during the raids suggests the scam network may have targeted foreign nationals as well. The CBI has alerted overseas law enforcement agencies through Interpol channels, sharing intelligence on the modus operandi, domain registration patterns, and the money trail. The international dimension adds significant complexity, requiring coordination with multiple foreign jurisdictions.

Operation Chakra-VI builds on earlier phases: Chakra I-III targeted fraudulent call centres, while Chakra IV-V focused on illegal payment gateways and mule accounts. The CBI has indicated that further arrests are expected as forensic analysis of seized devices continues, and that Operation Chakra-VII is already in the planning stages.
