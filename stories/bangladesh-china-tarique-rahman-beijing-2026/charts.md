# Charts — Bangladesh-China Pivot 2026

## 1. Trade Comparison: China vs India in Bangladesh (2025)
- **Type**: Grouped bar chart or side-by-side comparison
- **Data**:
  - China-Bangladesh bilateral trade: $27.4B
  - India-Bangladesh bilateral trade: $15.6B
  - Bangladesh exports to China: $5.2B
  - Bangladesh exports to India: $3.4B
  - Bangladesh imports from China: $22.2B
  - Bangladesh imports from India: $12.2B
- **Insight**: China is now Bangladesh's largest trading partner, surpassing India. The gap has widened significantly since 2019.

## 3. Timeline: Bangladesh's Foreign Policy Shift (2011–2026)
- **Type**: Horizontal timeline with milestone markers
- **Key events**:
  - 2011: Teesta water-sharing treaty falls through after Mamata Banerjee objects
  - 2014: Bangladesh joins China's Belt and Road Initiative
  - 2016: Xi Jinping visits Dhaka; $38B in deals signed
  - 2018: India commits to Mongla port development
  - 2022: China surpasses India as Bangladesh's largest trading partner
  - 2023: Bangladesh invites China to bid on Mongla port
  - 2024: Hasina government falls; student protests; Tarique Rahman elected
  - 2025: China commits $430M to Mongla; Tarique signals foreign policy rebalancing
  - Jun 22-26, 2026: Tarique Rahman Beijing visit; 16-point communiqué signed

## 5. Teesta River Basin Strategic Map
- **Type**: Topographic/geographic map with key chokepoints
- **Features**:
  - River course: Sikkim → Siliguri Corridor → Bangladesh
  - Siliguri Corridor width: 22km at narrowest point
  - Distance from proposed Chinese project site to Indian border
  - Proximity to India's Northeast states
- **Insight**: The geostrategic vulnerability is unparalleled — Chinese infrastructure at India's narrowest territorial point.
