# India-US After G7: What Actually Changed

## Cold Open

On Tuesday, Prime Minister Narendra Modi met President Donald Trump at the G7 Summit in Evian-les-Bains — their first face-to-face meeting in 16 months. The handshake was warm, the conversation short, and one thing was conspicuously missing: the embrace that defined their earlier relationship. Behind the diplomatic optics, five substantive issues dominated the agenda, and the real deadline has now shifted to July 24. Here is what actually changed — and what both sides are missing.

## The Meeting

Modi arrived in Evian for his seventh consecutive G7 appearance, invited by French President Emmanuel Macron. While his schedule included bilaterals with the UK, Canada, and UAE, all eyes were on the Modi-Trump meeting. Their last formal bilateral was in February 2025 at the White House shortly after Trump's second inauguration. In the 16 months since: 50 per cent tariffs on Indian goods, the Settebello incident that killed three Indian sailors, and no resolution on the trade front.

## Five Issues on the Table

**The Trade Deal.** US Ambassador Sergio Gor says only one per cent of the interim agreement remains unresolved. But that one per cent is everything — market access for American dairy, medical devices, and agricultural products, India's most protected sectors.

**The Dead Sailors.** On June 10, US forces struck three tankers in the Gulf of Oman. Three Indian sailors died. India summoned the US charge d'affaires — a rare and serious diplomatic move.

**Tariffs.** The US imposed a 25 per cent tariff on Indian goods plus a penalty linked to India's Russian oil purchases. A new Section 301 investigation could add another 12.5 per cent.

**H-1B Visas.** Indian tech workers face tighter restrictions. No specifics announced, but the signal is clear.

**The US-Iran Deal.** The Strait of Hormuz reopening and falling oil prices weaken India's argument for buying Russian oil and give Trump more leverage.

## What Actually Changed

**The hug is gone.** The relationship has shifted from personal chemistry to hard bargaining. That is not necessarily bad — it might be more honest.

**India is building options.** Modi signed a deal with the EU, deepened ties with France, thawed relations with China. Strategic autonomy in action.

**The deadline moved.** The real deadline is now July 24 — when the universal 10 per cent tariff expires. USTR Jamieson Greer flies to Delhi on June 22.

**The US-Iran deal changed the math.** Lower energy costs benefit India, but Trump gains leverage. It is a recalibration, not a win-lose.

**The real work happens in Delhi.** The Greer-Goyal meeting on June 22-24 will lock in the actual terms — not the photo op at the G7.

## What Both Sides Are Missing

India's narrative: "America is being unfair." America's narrative: "India is dragging its feet." Both are right — and both are incomplete. India has real leverage as the world's fastest-growing major economy. The US has real leverage through tariff power and technology access. The question is whether both sides can use their leverage without breaking the relationship.

## What to Watch

- June 22-24: USTR Greer in Delhi for final negotiations
- July 7: Section 301 hearings on forced labour tariffs
- July 24: Universal 10 per cent tariff expires — the hard deadline
- Mid-July: Target for signing the interim framework
