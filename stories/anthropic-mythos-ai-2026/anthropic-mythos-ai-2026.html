Anthropic Mythos AI: The Model That Could Hack Anything — Now in Safe Mode

In April 2026, Anthropic released an AI model so capable at cybersecurity that the company refused to let the public use it. Mythos Preview could find vulnerabilities in decades-old code in minutes, exploit them autonomously, and generate working attacks faster than most human hackers. In June, Anthropic released a safe version called Fable 5 — and then, three days later, suspended access to both models. This is the story of the most capable — and most restricted — AI model ever built for the public.

What Is Mythos?

Mythos is not a single model. It is a new capability tier — above the Opus class — in Anthropic's Claude lineup. The first Mythos-class model, Mythos Preview, launched April 7, 2026 through Project Glasswing, a collaboration between Anthropic, the US government, and 12 technology companies including Amazon, Apple, Google, Microsoft, NVIDIA, Cisco, CrowdStrike, and JPMorganChase. The project's mission: use Mythos's offensive capabilities to find and patch critical vulnerabilities in the world's most important software before attackers could exploit them.

On June 9, Anthropic released two models simultaneously: Claude Fable 5, a Mythos-class model made safe for general use through safety classifiers, and Claude Mythos 5, the same underlying model with those classifiers lifted, available only to Glasswing partners. The naming is deliberate — "the name difference reflects the safeguard difference, not a capability difference," Anthropic said.

What Makes It Different

Mythos represents a step change in two domains: cybersecurity and scientific research.

Cybersecurity. Anthropic's research team tested Mythos Preview against 18 Firefox patches and 21 Windows kernel vulnerabilities. The model autonomously developed working exploits for 8 of 18 Firefox vulnerabilities — producing its first exploit within one hour of the patch becoming available. Firefox's official fix was still 18 days from release. Against Windows, Mythos produced proof-of-concept exploits for 18 of 21 vulnerabilities, triggering a Blue Screen of Death in each case. It generated all 18 within six hours. The first took 31 minutes.

"N-day has become dangerously misnamed," the researchers concluded. "N-hour is closer to the reality we now operate in."

Biology. In blinded head-to-head comparisons, Anthropic's scientists preferred Mythos's molecular biology hypotheses ~80% of the time over Opus-class models. One Mythos-generated hypothesis — a novel mechanism for an E. coli protein — was independently corroborated by a separate lab working on the same problem. The model also conducted novel research autonomously over a week: it assembled single-cell RNA sequencing data for millions of cells across 38 animal species and designed a custom machine learning model to identify homologous cells across distantly related organisms.

Mythos Preview could weaponize disclosed vulnerabilities in minutes, not days — compressing a process that historically took weeks into hours

Project Glasswing: The Gatekeepers

Anthropic did not release Mythos into the wild. Instead, it created Project Glasswing — an initiative bringing together the US government and 12 major technology companies to use Mythos's capabilities defensively. The model scans critical infrastructure software for vulnerabilities that human engineers had missed, sometimes for decades. In one case, Mythos found a bug that had lain dormant in a system for 27 years.

The UK's AI Security Institute (AISI) evaluated Mythos Preview and confirmed it could "execute multi-stage attacks on vulnerable networks and discover and exploit vulnerabilities autonomously — tasks that would take human professionals days of work." But AISI also cautioned that its test environments lacked real-world defenses like active monitoring and incident response. "We cannot say for sure whether Mythos Preview would be able to attack well-defended systems," the report concluded.

"A lone operator can now turn a month's worth of patches into working exploits in a single afternoon — for a few thousand dollars and with no specialized expertise."

Fable 5: Mythos for Everyone

On June 9, Anthropic launched Fable 5 — the first Mythos-class model available to the general public. The model uses safety classifiers to detect and block sensitive queries in cybersecurity, biology, chemistry, and AI distillation. When a query triggers these classifiers, the model falls back to Opus 4.8, a less capable model. The company ran a bug bounty program to test these safeguards; no one found a complete jailbreak.

But Fable 5 came with strings. All traffic requires 30-day data retention, even for customers who previously had zero-retention agreements — a policy that could set an industry precedent. Pricing was set at $10 per million input tokens and $50 per million output tokens, double the price of Opus 4.8. And on June 12 — three days after launch — Anthropic suspended access to both Fable 5 and Mythos 5, citing "unexpected demand and infrastructure constraints."

The IPO Context

The Mythos launch coincided with Anthropic's confidential IPO filing, first reported by Fortune. The company is preparing to enter public markets alongside OpenAI and Elon Musk's SpaceX. The timing raises questions: was the dual-model strategy — a safe public version and an unrestricted partner version — designed to reassure regulators ahead of the IPO? Anthropic says the safety measures predate the filing, but the convergence is notable.

Days before the launch, Anthropic also published a plea urging major AI labs to establish a coordinated "brake pedal" on frontier AI development. The company warned that systems are advancing so rapidly they may soon achieve recursive self-improvement (RSI) — autonomously improving themselves without human intervention.

The Safety Debate

Ciaran Martin, former head of the UK's National Cyber Security Centre, said Mythos's ability to find vulnerabilities "has really shaken people." The White House has since established a framework to test the most powerful AI models before release. Mythos 5 is deployed in collaboration with the US government — a first for a frontier AI model.

But critics question whether the safety narrative serves Anthropic's commercial interests. By positioning Mythos as too dangerous for general release, the company signals to the market that it has the most powerful technology — while still offering a version (Fable 5) that generates revenue. "The bifurcation of Mythos into a fully capable model and a guardrailed version for general use is reasonable," noted DeepLearning.AI, "while security teams continue their work."

The Bottom Line

THE VERDICT

Mythos is the first AI model whose creators judged it too capable to release without a safety bypass switch. The technology itself is a genuine leap — the difference between Mythos and previous models is not incremental but categorical, especially in cybersecurity. The dual-model strategy (Fable + Mythos) is a reasonable compromise between openness and safety, but the suspension three days after launch reveals how fragile the infrastructure for safe AI deployment still is. Anthropic has set a new bar — both for capability and for the precautions required to deploy it. Whether other labs follow that standard or race past it will define the next phase of the AI era.
