Mohan Yadav Land Scam: CM Family Bought 168 Acres in Ujjain Growth Zones

An Indian Express investigation reveals how MP Chief Minister Mohan Yadav's family acquired 137 plots spanning 168 acres worth ₹45 crore — almost entirely in zones his government was about to develop. The BJP had warned him before he took office.

The Bottom Line

A landmark Indian Express investigation has revealed that MP Chief Minister Mohan Yadav's family acquired 168 acres across 137 plots worth ₹45 crore in Ujjain — mostly in zones his own government designated for infrastructure development — raising the most serious conflict-of-interest questions to hit a BJP state leader since the Vyapam era.

A City Transformed — and a Family That Got There First

Ujjain, the ancient temple city on the banks of the Shipra, is in the middle of the largest infrastructure overhaul in its history. Eight major highway projects radiate outward from the city. A ₹700-crore bypass is under construction. The Ujjain Master Plan 2035 has rezoned hundreds of acres from agricultural to residential and commercial use. The catalyst: Simhastha Kumbh 2028, a religious gathering expected to draw 35–40 crore devotees — more than triple the 7 crore who came in 2016.

But amid this urban renewal, one family's land portfolio has grown at a pace that defies ordinary coincidence. An Indian Express investigation, published June 23, 2026, found that Chief Minister Mohan Yadav's family and their real estate companies bought at least 137 plots spanning 168 acres for ₹45 crore in Ujjain between December 2023 and December 2025 — almost entirely in zones that his own government's projects and master plan were about to make dramatically more valuable.

The pattern of acquisitions reveals a striking geographic correlation. Of the 168 acres purchased, 111 acres lie directly adjacent to highway and road projects announced by Yadav's government after he became CM.

Ujjain District Collector Roushan Singh confirmed to the Indian Express that “over half a dozen” highway projects — new construction or lane expansion — were underway to improve connectivity to Garoth, Jhalawar, Nagda, Badnawar, Indore, and Bhopal, with “another 15–20 internal road widening works” in the pipeline.

The Master Plan Connection

The Ujjain Master Plan 2035, released in May 2023 — months before Yadav became CM — rezoned hundreds of acres from agricultural to residential and commercial use. The Yadav family's land holdings overlap with nearly every zone in Ujjain where land use was changed.

The timing was notable: weeks before the Master Plan's release, the family purchased at least 30 acres in Sawarakhedi in March–April 2023. Since Yadav became CM, the family acquired at least 37 additional acres in newly reclassified zones — including the 18 acres in Pandyakhedi, now designated as “commercial.”

In 2025, even as the Madhya Pradesh government was grappling with fierce local resistance to its proposal for pooling farmland to build permanent Kumbh infrastructure, Yadav's family and their companies purchased at least 62 plots spanning 92 acres. The government eventually revoked the land pooling scheme in November 2025 after farmer protests.

The Pre-Warnings: BJP Knew

The most damaging revelation in the South First report is that the BJP's own top leadership knew about Yadav's land interests — and warned him.

“It is in your interests that you stay away from real-estate activity.”

BJP national observers to Mohan Yadav — before he became CM

Senior BJP leaders who oversaw Madhya Pradesh operations for the national leadership confirmed that the caution was conveyed while Yadav was still a minister in the Shivraj Singh Chouhan government. The advice, according to South First, “fell on deaf ears” — the family's pace of acquisition only accelerated after he took office.

The report also raised a more uncomfortable question: several of the accused in the infamous Vyapam scam that rocked the Chouhan government were said to be followers of Mohan Yadav. Yet the BJP chose him to replace Chouhan.

The Defence: Real Estate ‘Since 2010’

Neither Chief Minister Yadav nor his office responded to The Indian Express's detailed questionnaire. However, multiple responses emerged from officials and family:

“Our family is in realty business since 2010 when my father developed a 100-bigha property. My uncle has many housing projects. As private individuals, we have every right to buy, develop or sell any private land. Should we shut down just because the CM is from our family?”

Anant Yadav, nephew (son of cousin Govind)

State government officials, speaking anonymously, offered two defences:

  1. Extended family separation: “When you talk about his family, it should include only his wife, son and daughter-in-law… and there you may not find anything major.”
  2. Business continuity: “The CM's extended family has been in the real estate business for a while. To link their businesses with the CM's rise in politics will not be proper.”

On the Gangedi project specifically, Anant Yadav claimed the land deals “date back to 2020 when he (Mohan Yadav) was not even a minister” and that “the highway work was cleared back in 2019.” The land, he said, was “100m from the highway.”

The Counter-Argument: A Pattern Too Precise to Ignore

Congress leaders were unsparing. State president Jitu Patwari demanded Yadav's resignation and a Supreme Court-led judicial probe, calling it the “loot of Mahakal land.”

“Facts clearly show that the BJP is involved in the theft of Ram Temple donations and the looting of Mahakal land.”

Jitu Patwari, MP Congress President

AICC general secretary Jairam Ramesh took to X, calling it an “engine of loot running at full speed.” Leader of the Opposition Umang Singhar coined the phrase “Mohan Yadav Files” — invoking the Kerala Files and Epstein Files.

The structural critique is straightforward: Yadav was Ujjain Development Authority chairman from 2004–2010. He has been the local MLA since 2013. He oversaw infrastructure and tourism development in Ujjain for decades. The Master Plan 2035 was shaped during his tenure. The road projects were announced by his government. And his family bought land in precisely the zones and corridors that these official actions would benefit.

What Happens Next

The controversy puts Prime Minister Modi in an awkward position. As the South First editorial noted, “for the first time, Modi is faced with a situation where his ministers are seen to be no different from the ones who were caught in controversies during the previous Congress regime.”

Three threads to watch:

  1. BJP internal response: Will the central leadership act, or will Yadav be allowed to ride it out? The precedent of Shivraj Singh Chouhan — dropped as CM despite four terms — suggests no one is irreplaceable.
  2. Congress agitation: The party has announced it will raise the issue in the Madhya Pradesh assembly and has demanded a Supreme Court-monitored probe. Whether this gains traction depends on whether more documents surface.
  3. Media scrutiny: The Indian Express investigation is Part 2 of a series. Parts 3–5 — including details of how cousins flipped land for profit and the full roster of family companies — suggest more revelations may follow.

The Verdict

This is not a story about a politician who owns land. It is a story about a Chief Minister whose family systematically acquired 168 acres in precisely the locations his own government was making more valuable — and did so after being warned by his own party to stop. Whether this constitutes corruption or merely poor judgment will be decided by investigators. But the pattern, as documented by Indian Express, is damning.
