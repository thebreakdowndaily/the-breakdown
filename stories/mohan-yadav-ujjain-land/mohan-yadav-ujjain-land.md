## THE BOTTOM LINE

An Indian Express investigation published June 23, 2026 found that Madhya Pradesh Chief Minister Mohan Yadav's family acquired 168 acres across 137 plots worth â‚¹45 crore in Ujjain between December 2023 and December 2025 — almost entirely in zones his own government's projects and master plan were about to make dramatically more valuable. Of the 168 acres, 111 lie directly adjacent to highway and road projects announced by Yadav's government after he became CM. The BJP's own top leadership had warned Yadav to stay away from real estate before he assumed office. He did not.

## The Family Network

The acquisitions were spread across an extended network: CM Mohan Yadav holds 17 acres directly plus 39.5 via Siddhi Vinayak Devcon (73% stake with wife Seema Yadav, who holds 10.6 acres directly). Son Vaibhav Yadav owns 16 acres. Daughter-in-law Shalini Yadav (married Feb 2024) bought 10+ acres in Gangedi in Jun-Jul 2025. Cousin Govind (Bablu) Yadav acquired 46 acres since 2024, developed with Shanti Mahalok Builders. Cousin Nilesh Yadav acquired 78 acres since 2024 across 4 RERA-registered Sawariya housing schemes. Brothers Nandlal and Narayan Yadav purchased through personal and company holdings. Nephew Abhay Yadav acquired 7.4 acres via Shrivenktesh Devbuild LLP in July 2025.

## The Master Plan Connection

The Ujjain Master Plan 2035, released in May 2023, rezoned hundreds of acres from agricultural to residential and commercial. Weeks before its release, the family purchased at least 30 acres in Sawarakhedi. Since Yadav became CM, the family acquired at least 37 additional acres in newly reclassified zones — including 18 acres in Pandyakhedi, now designated commercial. Ujjain District Collector Roushan Singh confirmed "over half a dozen" highway projects underway with "another 15-20 internal road widening works" in the pipeline.

## The Pre-Warnings

BJP national observers told Yadav before he became CM: "It is in your interests that you stay away from real-estate activity." Senior BJP leaders confirmed the caution was conveyed while Yadav was still a minister. The advice "fell on deaf ears" — the family's pace of acquisition only accelerated after he took office on December 13, 2023.

## The Defense

Nephew Anant Yadav stated: "Our family is in realty business since 2010. As private individuals, we have every right to buy, develop or sell any private land. Should we shut down just because the CM is from our family?" State government officials, speaking anonymously, argued that scrutiny should be limited to the CM's "wife, son and daughter-in-law."

## The Counter-Argument

Congress president Jitu Patwari demanded Yadav's resignation and a Supreme Court-led judicial probe. The structural critique: Yadav chaired the Ujjain Development Authority from 2004-2010, has been the local MLA since 2013, the Master Plan 2035 was shaped during his tenure, the road projects were announced by his government, and his family bought land in precisely the zones these official actions would benefit.

## What Happens Next

BJP internal response: will the central leadership act? Congress has announced it will raise the issue in the assembly and has demanded a Supreme Court-monitored probe. The Indian Express investigation is Part 2 of a series; Parts 3-5 include details of how cousins flipped land for profit. Whether this constitutes corruption or merely poor judgment will be decided by investigators, but the pattern — 168 acres acquired after a warning to stop, in zones the government was simultaneously upgrading — is documented.
