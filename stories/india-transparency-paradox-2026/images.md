# Visual Intelligence Spec — India's Transparency Paradox

## Hero Image
- **Subject**: A citizen in a modest rural or semi-urban setting holding a paper document (RTI application) with a government office building in soft focus background
- **Mood**: Quiet determination, dignity, bureaucratic weight
- **Lighting**: Natural daylight, slightly overcast — noon in India
- **Color palette**: Muted earth tones with a single warm accent (the saffron/gold stamp on the RTI document)
- **Composition**: Rule of thirds — subject on the left, document filling the lower-right frame, out-of-focus government signage in deep background
- **Tone**: Documentary, not dramatic
- **Symbolism**: The paper as a fragile but powerful tool against an imposing institution

## Timeline Graphic: 20 Years of Transparency — Key Events
- **Type**: Vertical timeline with alternating left-right markers
- **Events**:
  - 2005: RTI Act passed unanimously by Parliament
  - 2006: Right to Information comes into force (October 12)
  - 2011: Anna Hazare anti-corruption movement
  - 2013: Lokpal and Lokayuktas Act passed
  - 2014: Lokpal Act comes into force (January 16)
  - 2017: Electoral Bonds scheme introduced (Finance Act)
  - 2019: RTI Act amended — commissioner independence reduced
  - 2023: DPDP Act passed — amends RTI §8(1)(j)
  - 2024: Supreme Court strikes down Electoral Bonds (February 15)
  - 2025: CPI global average drops for first time in a decade
  - 2026: RTI turns 22 — 4.13 lakh appeals pending; CIC has 2 of 11 commissioners
- **Color coding**: Green (pro-transparency), Red (setback), Gold (Supreme Court action)
