# Visual Intelligence Plan — India's Monsoon Crisis 2026

> Visual Editor's brief: Every graphic must answer a question. Readers should understand the story even if they only skim the graphics.

---

## 1. HERO VISUAL — "The Parched Earth"

**Concept:** Cinematic editorial photograph of a farmer standing on cracked, sun-baked earth in Maharashtra or Gujarat, looking at an empty horizon. The sky is hazy with dust, not rain clouds. A single wilted cotton or soybean plant in the foreground.

**Mood:** Grim, anticipatory, dignified — not melodramatic. The farmer is not weeping; he is assessing.

**Color Palette:** Desaturated ochre, burnt sienna, pale sky — reflecting the lack of moisture. Avoid blue skies (ironic).

**Composition:** Rule of thirds — farmer on left third, cracked earth dominating lower two-thirds, empty sky filling upper third.

**Symbolism:** The crack patterns in the earth should naturally suggest fractals — the interconnected nature of the crisis.

**Licensing:** Reuters photo library (e.g., Sahiba Chawdhary's Kolkata image for reference mood), or commissioned photography. Alternative: Creative Commons search for "India drought farmer cracked earth" with proper attribution.

**Caption:** *"A farmer surveys his parched field in Maharashtra, where June rainfall was 85% below normal — the worst deficit of any Indian state in the 2026 monsoon season."*

## 2. TIMELINE — "The Monsoon That Stalled"

**Type:** Vertical/snake chronological timeline with milestone markers

**Question answered:** How did we get here?

**Key Milestones:**
- **Apr 15:** IMD first forecast: 92% of LPA (below normal)
- **May 29:** IMD downgrade to 90% of LPA; 60% chance of deficient; El Niño confirmed
- **Jun 1-18:** Monsoon stalls over southern Maharashtra (11-day pause)
- **Jun 12:** Govt imposes fuel sale restrictions (West Asia supply disruption)
- **Jun 20:** Deficit peaks at 46% (45.6mm vs 84.4mm normal)
- **Jun 22:** India Today calls it "driest June of 21st century"
- **Jun 25:** Kharif sowing data shows 22.7% decline YoY
- **Jun 30:** IMD announces below-average July forecast; June confirmed 3rd driest on record
- **Jul 1:** LPG price cut (₹183.50); fuel restrictions lifted

**Visual elements:** Icons for each milestone (cloud, thermometer, plant, fuel pump, chart, calendar). Connecting line colored gold (#D4A843) on dark background (#0A0A0A).

## 3. CHART — "126 Years of June Rainfall"

**Type:** Vertical bar chart (1901-2026)

**Question answered:** How unprecedented is this June's rainfall?

**Data:** 126 bars — each representing June rainfall for every year from 1901 to 2026. The 2026 bar is highlighted in red (#D4A843 gold). 2009 (87.5mm) and 1905 (92.3mm) bars in dark orange.

**Reference line:** LPA of ~165mm in dashed white/grey.

**Annotations:** Callout for 2009 (lowest), 1905 (2nd), 2026 (3rd), and normal.

**Insight:** The three lowest bars all cluster below 100mm. Most years cluster between 120-200mm. The visual immediately shows 2026 as an extreme outlier.

**Type:** Choropleth map of India's 36 meteorological subdivisions

**Question answered:** Where is the rain missing most?

**Color scale:** Deep red (-85%) through orange, yellow, pale green to dark green (+29%).

**Key annotations:**
- Maharashtra: -85% (deepest red)
- Gujarat: -84%
- Madhya Pradesh: -58%
- Chhattisgarh: -71%
- Jharkhand: -71%
- Rajasthan (east): +29% (anomalous green — exception)

**Inset box:** Table of top 5 deficit and top 2 surplus states.

**Data source:** IMD district rainfall bulletin, June 4-22 cumulative.

## 7. LINE CHART — "El Niño Index Trajectory"

**Type:** Line chart showing Nino 3.4 anomaly from Jan 2025 to forecast Sep 2026

**Question answered:** Is El Niño going to get worse?

**Data points:**
- Jan 2026: ~+0.3°C (neutral)
- May 2026: ~+0.8°C (transitioning)
- Jun 22, 2026: ~+1.7°C (El Niño)
- Forecast Jul-Sep 2026: strengthening

**Threshold lines:** +0.5°C (El Niño threshold), +1.5°C (strong El Niño), +2.0°C (very strong)

**Shading:** Green (neutral) → Yellow (El Niño watch) → Orange (El Niño) → Red (strong El Niño)
