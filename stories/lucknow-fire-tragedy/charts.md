# Charts & Data Visualizations — Lucknow Fire Tragedy

## Chart 1: India Fire Deaths Over Time (NCRB Data)
- **Type:** Line chart
- **Data:** 2015-2024
  - 2015: 17,700 deaths, 18,450 incidents
  - 2016: 17,400 deaths, 17,200 incidents
  - 2017: 16,800 deaths, 16,500 incidents
  - 2018: 16,200 deaths, 15,800 incidents
  - 2019: 15,500 deaths, 15,000 incidents
  - 2020: 12,800 deaths, 12,500 incidents
  - 2021: 10,600 deaths, 10,400 incidents
  - 2022: 8,900 deaths, 8,700 incidents
  - 2023: 6,900 deaths, 6,900 incidents
  - 2024: 5,888 deaths, 5,971 incidents
- **Source:** NCRB Accidental Deaths & Suicides in India
- **Insight:** 68% decline in incidents, 67% decline in deaths over a decade. But 16 people still die daily.

## Chart 2: Fire Deaths by Location (2024)
- **Type:** Pie chart
- **Data:**
  - Residential buildings: 3,555 (60.4%)
  - Commercial buildings: 277 (4.7%)
  - Factories: 149 (2.5%)

- **Insight:** Residential buildings account for 6 out of every 10 fire deaths in India.

## Chart 3: Fire Deaths by Cause (2024)
- **Type:** Horizontal bar chart
- **Data:**
  - Cooking gas cylinder/stove bursts: 23.6%
  - Electrical short circuits: 17.2%
  - Fireworks: 2.3%
  - Other/Unknown: 57.0%
- **Source:** NCRB 2024, Beyond Carlton analysis
- **Insight:** Everyday household activities — cooking and electricity — account for nearly 40% of fire deaths.

## Chart 5: The 15-Metre Threshold Impact
- **Type:** Comparison table (in-story)
- **Data:**
  - UP: 15m threshold — buildings below exempt from fire NOC — No reform yet
  - Delhi: 15m (17.5m with stilt) — ~95% buildings exempt — Under review
  - Telangana: 15m — millions of buildings outside fire dept purview — Amendment proposed
  - NBC 2016: 15m — low-rise classification, minimal requirements — Baseline standard
- **Source:** State fire services acts, NBC 2016, HT, TOI
- **Insight:** The 15-metre threshold is the single most important number in India's fire safety framework — and it leaves the majority of urban buildings unprotected.
