## Cold Open

On June 23, 2026, Prime Minister Narendra Modi met President Droupadi Murmu at Rashtrapati Bhavan, setting in motion what is expected to be the most consequential reorganisation of his third-term council of ministers. Hours earlier, George Kurian, the Minister of State for Minority Affairs, resigned — the first domino in a sequence that closely follows the pattern Modi established with reshuffles in July 2016 and July 2021.

This time the stakes are considerably higher: the reshuffle is widely expected to be the last major reorganisation before the 2029 general elections, and it comes at a moment when the BJP is simultaneously managing a historic influx of defectors from opposition parties, preparing for crucial assembly elections due in 2027, and signalling intent to consolidate power ahead of 2029.

## The Reshuffle

The process began in mid-June. On June 16, Harsh Malhotra was appointed president of the Delhi BJP unit, requiring his eventual removal from the Union council. On June 18, the BJP announced its Rajya Sabha candidate list, notably excluding Ravneet Bittu and George Kurian — signalling their ministerial careers were drawing to a close. Their Rajya Sabha terms expired on June 21. On June 24, the Union Cabinet met in what was widely interpreted as a preparatory session for the broader reorganisation. The reshuffle is expected to be formally announced before the Monsoon Session of Parliament in July.

## Key Appointments

The reshuffle is driven largely by the need to accommodate a historic wave of defectors. In April 2026, seven Aam Aadmi Party Rajya Sabha MPs — including Raghav Chadha, former cricketer Harbhajan Singh, and Sandeep Pathak — merged with the BJP, and all are expected to receive ministerial positions. In June 2026, 20 TMC Lok Sabha rebels merged with the NCPI and signalled intent to join the NDA; Kakoli Ghosh Dastidar and Sudip Bandyopadhyay are tipped for berths. Six Shiv Sena (UBT) MPs joined the Shinde camp in what has been dubbed "Operation Tiger."

The reshuffle is also expected to address the current gender imbalance — only seven of the current 72 ministers are women — and to introduce younger faces to appeal to first-time voters ahead of 2029.

## Portfolio Changes

The most significant portfolio change under discussion is the potential elevation of Piyush Goyal from Commerce to Finance, replacing Nirmala Sitharaman, who has held the portfolio since 2019. Ashwini Vaishnaw, currently Minister of Railways and Electronics and IT, may be moved to Commerce. Dharmendra Pradhan's position is understood to be uncertain following the NEET paper leak controversy. Devendra Fadnavis is being discussed for a possible return to the Centre. Several Ministers of State are expected to be dropped for underperformance — the Prime Minister's Office has compiled detailed performance data on every minister.

## Political Significance

This reshuffle is driven by three interconnected forces. First, the need to accommodate the largest influx of defectors from opposition parties in Indian political history, rewarding them while integrating them into the NDA's governance framework. Second, the imperative to signal intent for the 2027 assembly elections in Uttar Pradesh, Punjab, Gujarat, Uttarakhand, Goa, Manipur, and Himachal Pradesh — elections that will serve as a semi-final before 2029. Third, the need to build a team capable of delivering a fourth consecutive term for Modi, combining performance orientation, regional representation, and demographic appeal. The reshuffle is, in essence, the beginning of the 2029 campaign.
