## CAPTION

India's biggest rural employment scheme was replaced TODAY — after 21 years.

MGNREGA → VB-G RAM G.

Here is what changes:

→ **125 days** guaranteed (up from 100)
→ **₹300/day** minimum wage floor (statutory — first time ever)
→ States now pay **40%** of wage costs (was 0%)
→ **21 states** got a wage increase — some up to 25%
→ National average wage: **₹327.4/day** (↑9.5%)
→ **₹95,692 Cr** budget allocation (highest ever)

The wage floor matters most.

Bihar was paying ₹210/day. UP was ₹230/day. Jharkhand, West Bengal, MP, Rajasthan — all below ₹300. All now raised to ₹300. That is a 30-43% jump for the lowest-paid workers.

But here is the trade-off: states that could not afford ₹210 now have to pay ₹300 — and cover 40% of the cost themselves.

Five opposition states — Karnataka, Kerala, Punjab, Telangana, Jharkhand — have passed resolutions against it. Twenty-five states have allocated funds.

The old scheme ran from 2005 to yesterday. The new one starts today.

This is the single largest rural policy shift in two decades.
