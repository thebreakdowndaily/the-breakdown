Phase 0

Account Architecture

Before a single post, lock down every setting. These decisions affect reach, monetization, and discoverability.

1

Account Type

Creator vs Business

Creator Account is the right choice. Why: cleaner profile (no "Business" label), access to Creator Insights, growth & inspiration tabs, easier brand partnership tools. Switch before posting.

Pro tip: Business accounts get less organic reach. Stay on Creator until you're running ads.
2

Category & Contact

Set once, don't touch again

Go to Edit Profile → Category. Choose the most specific option:

  • Journalist (best) → shows "Journalist" below name, builds credibility
  • News & Media → alternative if Journalist isn't available
  • Add hello@thebreakdown.in as contact email
  • Turn on "Action Button" → set to Email or Newsletter link
Warning: If you choose "Digital Creator" you lose the News/Media context. Your audience needs to know you're analysis, not lifestyle.
3

Handle & Display Name

Username: thebreakdowndaily

  • Display name: The Breakdown (not @handle, not THEBREAKDOWN)
  • Capitalization in display matters: "The Breakdown" reads better than "THE BREAKDOWN"
  • Keep handle short. @thebreakdowndaily works
  • No underscores, no numbers except "daily"
4

Profile Photo

The B Icon Mark

Export from logo pack. Specifications:

  • Square, 320x320px minimum
  • Background: #1a1a2e (charcoal)
  • B letter: #e4a11d (gold), bold sans-serif
  • 2px gold border around the circle
  • Test at 32x32px — must still read as "B"
  • No text, no gradient, no shadow
Why: Instagram displays PFP as a circle at ~40px in feed. Complex icons vanish at that size. The B mark survives.
5

Bio Architecture

3 lines + 1 CTA

A bio is a landing page. Every word competes for attention. Here's the exact formula:

  • Line 1 (Tagline): Complex stories. Clear analysis.
  • Line 2 (Categories): Geopolitics | Tech | Economy | Media
  • Line 3 (Schedule): New breakdowns every Tue & Fri ↓
  • Line 4 (Link): linktr.ee/thebreakdown (or direct to website)

Full bio as it should appear:

Complex stories. Clear analysis.
Geopolitics | Tech | Economy | Media
New breakdowns every Tue & Fri ↓
linktr.ee/thebreakdown
Bio do's: Line breaks in bio = press Enter. Use the ↓ arrow (↓) to hint at link. No emoji overload — max 2. One clear CTA.
6

Link-in-Bio Strategy

Linktree vs Own Site

Use linktr.ee/thebreakdown for flexibility. Order:

  1. YouTube: Latest video (changes weekly)
  2. Newsletter: Subscribe page (static)
  3. X / Twitter: @thebreakdown
  4. Website: thebreakdown.in
10K followers unlock: Instagram allows native link in Stories. Until then, Linktree is your best option.
7

Notifications & Privacy

Settings to check now

  • Turn on push notifications for comments, DMs, mentions
  • Turn off "Show Activity Status" — prevents pressure to reply instantly
  • Turn on "Hide Like Counts" — healthier growth mindset
  • Enable two-factor authentication — non-negotiable
  • Connect Facebook Page — unlocks cross-posting, better insights
  • Turn off "Allow Tagging" from non-followers — avoids spam

Phase 1

Visual & Voice Identity

Every post is a brand impression. Consistency in design + tone = recognition. These systems ensure every post feels like The Breakdown.

Design System

Colors

#0f0f1a BG
#1a1a2e Card
#e4a11d Gold
#ffffff Text

Typography

Headlines: Bebas Neue, all caps, wide tracking (2-4px)
Body: Source Sans 3, clean, light (300) to semi-bold (600)
Emphasis: Italic Source Sans 3 for quotes / stats
Never use: Script, decorative, or display fonts

Design Rules

  • ✓ All posts use dark backgrounds (never white)
  • ✓ Gold accent on every slide (line, icon, or text)
  • ✓ B mark on all content (bottom-right, 10% opacity)
  • ✓ Never use stock photos. Graphics + icons only.
  • ✓ Max 3 font sizes per slide. Hierarchy is everything.
  • ✓ 8px or 12px grid for spacing. No floating elements.

Brand Voice

Tone Spectrum

Analytical Clear Sharp Confident
ContextToneDoDon't
Breaking NewsUrgent, factual"This just happened. Here's what it means.""OMG you won't believe this"
AnalysisCalm, authoritative"There are 3 reasons this matters.""Let me explain in simple terms" (condescending)
OpinionConfident, grounded"The data suggests otherwise.""In my humble opinion" (weak)
CommunityConversational, warm"What did we miss? Drop your take below.""Like if you agree" (begging)
RepliesDirect, respectful"Great point. Actually, the data shows...""You're wrong because..." (hostile)
Voice principle: Write like a smart friend explaining something complex — not like a textbook, not like a gossip.

Vocabulary Rules

  • Use "we" not "I" — builds community
  • Use "analysis" not "opinion" — frames authority
  • Use "story" not "post" — frames narrative
  • Replace "very" with stronger words (e.g., "critical" not "very important")
  • No emojis in analysis content. Reserve for stories and DMs.

Phase 7

Caption Architecture

Captions are reading copy, not greeting cards. Structure them for skimmers. Every caption follows the same formula.

📋 The Formula

Hook (1 line): Bold claim or question. Break line.

Body (2-4 lines): Key insight from the post. Add context. Leave them smarter after 5 seconds.

Question (1 line): Engagement prompt. End with "?"

CTA (1 line): Save / Follow / Share / Comment.

Line break
Hashtags: 3 primary + 5 category + 2 broad
Use this for every carousel

🔍 Carousel Example

India banned Telegram over a leaked exam paper. Here's what no one is talking about:

250 million users. 48 hours. One document.

The precedent this sets goes far beyond Telegram. Every global platform is watching.

What do you think — is the ban justified?

Save this for context. Follow @thebreakdowndaily for analysis.
176 characters (hook + body). Optimal length.

Engagement Engine (Daily SOP)

Morning (15 min)

  • → Reply to ALL comments from last 24h
  • → Reply to ALL DMs (even the "hey" ones)
  • → Like 15 posts from target accounts
  • → Comment on 5 target posts (value-add, not "nice")

Evening (10 min)

  • → Share 2-3 relevant stories from others
  • → Reply to any new comments
  • → Check analytics for 5 min
  • → DM 3 new followers with thanks

Comment Templates

On an analysis post:
"This is a solid breakdown. One angle worth adding: [insight]. What's your take on [related angle]?"

On a news post:
"Important context. Worth noting that [fact]. We covered this in more detail on our page."

On a data post:
"This is great data. Would be interesting to see [comparison]. Saved for reference."

Rule: Never comment just "Great post!" or an emoji. Add value or don't comment.
