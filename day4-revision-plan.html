TEACHING APTITUDE

**Levels:** Memory (rote) → Understanding (comprehension) → Reflective (critical)

**Bloom's:** R U A A E C (Remember, Understand, Apply, Analyze, Evaluate, Create)

**Evaluation:** Formative (during/feedback) vs Summative (after/grade)

**Methods:** Inductive (specific→general) vs Deductive (general→specific)

**Scholars:** Piaget (stages), Vygotsky (ZPD), Skinner (operant), Maslow (hierarchy)


RESEARCH APTITUDE

**Types:** Basic (knowledge) / Applied (solve) / Action (classroom) / Evaluative (assess)

**Probability Sampling:** SSSC — Simple, Stratified, Systematic, Cluster

**Non-Probability:** CPSQ — Convenience, Purposive, Snowball, Quota

**Hypothesis:** Null (H₀ = no relationship) vs Alternative (H₁ = relationship exists)

**Ethics:** Consent, Anonymity ≠ Confidentiality, IRB, Plagiarism


LOGICAL REASONING

**Square of Opposition:** A-E (contrary/both can't be true), I-O (sub-contrary/both can't be false), A-O & E-I (contradictory/must disagree)

**Indian Logic:** Vyapti (invariable relation), Hetu (reason), Hetvabhas (fallacy), Pratyaksha (perception), Anumana (inference)

**Fallacies:** Ad Hominem (person), Straw Man (misrepresent), False Dilemma (2 options), Slippery Slope (extreme outcome)


ICT

**Networks:** PAN → LAN → MAN → WAN

**Security:** Firewall, Encryption, VPN, Phishing, Malware, Ransomware

**MOOC:** SWAYAM, NPTEL, e-PG Pathshala, DIKSHA, Swayam Prabha (32 DTH channels)

**Memory:** RAM (volatile) vs ROM (non-volatile) | Byte = 8 bits


HIGHER EDUCATION

**NEP 2020:** 5+3+3+4 structure | Multi-entry/exit in UG | M.Phil phased out

**Bodies:** UGC (1956), AICTE (1987), NAAC (1994), NCTE (1995), ICSSR (1969)

    **Strategy:**

  • Round 1: Answer ALL easy questions (35-40)
  • Round 2: Attempt moderate ones (8-10)
  • Round 3: Leave or guess on very difficult (2-3)
  • **NO negative marking** → never leave blank; educated guess
  • Mark for review and come back

    • Paper II (100 Qs — 120 min):

    **Strategy:**

  • Round 1 (60 min): Answer all you're SURE of (~70 Qs)
  • Round 2 (40 min): Moderate difficulty (~20 Qs)
  • Round 3 (15 min): Difficult/unsure (~10 Qs — educated guess)
  • Round 4 (5 min): Review marked questions

    • **Question Type Tactic:**

  • **Direct questions:** Answer immediately or skip
  • **Assertion-Reason:** Cover options → decide if R explains A FIRST
  • **Match the Following:** Use elimination — match what you know, leave what you don't
  • **Statement questions:** Read BOTH statements fully before looking at options
  • **Chronology:** Find earliest and latest, then place middle

    • **Elimination Technique:**

  • 1. Eliminate obviously wrong options (usually 2)
  • 2. Compare remaining 2 carefully
  • 3. Look for absolute words (always, never, only) = usually false
  • 4. If both remaining seem correct → read the question again (you missed a nuance)

    • During Exam:

  • If you panic: close eyes, 3 deep breaths, open, continue
  • If you hit a hard question: mark and move — don't break rhythm
  • If you make a mistake: forget it, next question is fresh marks
  • Water sip after every 25 Qs
  • Last 5 min: ONLY check marked questions, don't change answers randomly

    • The Night Before:

  • NO new topics
  • NO difficult problems
  • Just REVISE the cheat sheets
  • Sleep at least 7 hours
  • Set alarm + backup alarm
  • Keep admit card + ID ready
