**High-Probability Topics (appear in 8+ of 10 exams):**
| Topic | Frequency | How NTA Asks |

| Agenda Setting (McCombs & Shaw) | Every exam | Scholar→Theory match |

**NTA Question Types in Theories:**
- **Direct:** "Who proposed Agenda Setting?" → McCombs & Shaw
- **Match the Following:** Column A (theory) ↔ Column B (scholar)
- **Statement-based:** "Assertion (A):... Reason (R):..." → decide
- **Comparison:** "How is Cultivation different from Agenda Setting?"
- **Chronology:** "Arrange theories in chronological order"

## NTA TREND SHIFTS (2019 → 2025)

| Period | Key Trends |
|--------|-----------|
| 2019–2021 | Heavy on direct theory definitions. Most Qs were one-line recall. |
| 2022 | Shift began: more match-the-following, statement-based. |
| 2023 | **Major shift:** Research ethics, AI & media, NEP 2020 questions surged. Indian Logic appeared more prominently. |
| 2024 | Scenario-based Paper I Qs became standard. Paper II had more assertion-reason formats. Chronology questions increased. |
| 2025 (June) | ICT weightage increased. Logical Reasoning had Indian Logic + Moods & Figures. Paper II had more media management and digital media Qs. |

**Overall Trend:** NTA is moving from *"what"* (definition recall) to *"why/how"* (application, analysis). Scenario-based and assertion-reason formats dominate.

## SMART STUDY STRATEGY FROM PYQ DATA

**Phase 1 (60% of time):** High-weightage units
- Paper I: Teaching + Research + Logical Reasoning
- Paper II: Theories + Laws + Research Methods
- Drill PYQs for these first

**Phase 2 (30% of time):** Moderate-weightage units
- Paper I: Communication, ICT, DI
- Paper II: Development Comm, Journalism History, ADPR

**Phase 3 (10% of time):** Low-weightage units
- Paper I: Environment, Higher Edu
- Paper II: Digital Media, Media Mgmt, International Comm

**Revision Rule:** Every topic you study → verify with 3 PYQs from different years. If NTA has asked it before, it WILL appear again.
