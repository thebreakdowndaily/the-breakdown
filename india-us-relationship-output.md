## A. NEWS SUMMARY

**Headline:** Modi and Trump Meet at G7 After 16 Months — Trade Deal Inches Forward, Sailor Deaths Cast Shadow

**100-word summary:** Prime Minister Narendra Modi and US President Donald Trump met face-to-face for the first time in 16 months at the G7 Summit in Evian, France. The two leaders exchanged a warm handshake — notably without Modi's signature hug — and held a brief conversation before their formal bilateral meeting. On the agenda: finalizing an interim trade deal (with just "1% left" according to US Ambassador Gor), addressing tensions over 3 Indian sailors killed in US strikes on tankers near the Strait of Hormuz, H-1B visa restrictions, and India's Russian oil purchases. While no final deal is expected to be signed at G7, US Trade Representative Jamieson Greer is scheduled to visit India on June 23-24 to give "final touches" to the framework agreement. The meeting marks a critical juncture in a relationship tested by tariffs, strategic differences, and competing interests.

**Key insight:** The relationship is entering a "mature phase" — personal chemistry still exists, but policy differences are real and structural. India is diversifying (deals with EU, France, China thaw) while the US demands market access and alignment on Russia/Iran. Neither side wants a break, but both are testing limits.
