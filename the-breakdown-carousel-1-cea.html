02 / 10 — Context
What Happened
India's Chief Economic Advisor V. Anantha Nageswaran said on an ANI podcast: "The globalising world gave advantage for software or MBA education — that era is over." He urged Indians to respect plumbers, carpenters, welders — skills AI cannot replace.
03 / 10 — Hidden Fact
The Hidden Fact
This isn't just opinion — it's policy signaling. India's top economic advisor is telling 1.5 crore college students that their parents' career plan is obsolete. That's not a hot take. That's a government position.

05 / 10 — Why It Matters
Why It Matters
India has no national trade certification. No social safety net for tradespeople. A cultural bias that treats "plumber" as failure. The CEA is right about the direction — but the infrastructure isn't ready.

07 / 10 — Contrarian
⚠️
The CEA is right about the problem. But telling students "become a plumber" without building trade certification, safety nets, and social respect is aspirational at best — abdication at worst.
08 / 10 — What Happens Next
What Happens Next
The degree bubble is bursting. But the "trade skill era" won't begin until India builds the bridge between aspiration and infrastructure. Germany didn't just say "learn trades" — they built a system. India needs to do the same.
