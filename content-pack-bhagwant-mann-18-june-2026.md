## FACT CHECK

### What's Verified
- A video exists showing a man resembling Bhagwant Mann splashing alcohol on portraits of Sikh Gurus and Jarnail Singh Bhindranwale
- Akal Takht summoned Mann in January 2026 for clarification
- Two government-recognized forensic labs submitted reports (May 27 and June 13, 2026) concluding the video is NOT AI-generated and NOT tampered with
- Akal Takht declared Mann "Guru Dokhi" and "Khalsa Panth Virodhi" on June 15, 2026
- The video was first posted by Canadian NRI Jagman Samra in October 2025
- AAP filed an FIR; a Mohali court ordered the video taken down
- AAP now claims two Haryana forensic labs (different from the ones Akal Takht used) certify the person is NOT Mann
- AAP says 1,191 frames were analyzed — facial structure, side profile, back profile, height, physical build all compared

### What's Unverified / Disputed
- Whether the person in the video is actually Bhagwant Mann — Akal Takht's labs say the video is authentic (implying it IS him), AAP's labs say it's NOT him
- Whether the video is AI-generated — Akal Takht says no, Mann initially claimed yes but now his own party's labs reportedly also say no (they say it's a real video of a different person)
- The identity of the person if it's NOT Mann — AAP hasn't named anyone
- The political motive — Mann alleges Sukhbir Singh Badal (SAD) orchestrated it; no independent evidence

### Key Unknown
Why two sets of government-recognized labs reached different conclusions. Both can't be right. This is the story's central tension.

### Fact-Check Verdict
**DISPUTED / TOO EARLY TO CALL.** Competing forensic claims from politically aligned sources. Independent third-party forensic audit needed. The AAP's claim (different person) and Akal Takht's claim (authentic video of Mann) are mutually exclusive. Until an independent authority reviews both, this remains a "he said, she said" with religious and political explosives attached.
