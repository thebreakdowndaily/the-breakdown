## 6. TIMELINE — Indian Union Budget & Tax Milestones

| Year | Event | Significance |
|------|-------|-------------|
| **1860** | James Wilson presents first Indian Budget | Introduced Income Tax to fund British administration post-1857 revolt |
| **1947-11-26** | R.K. Shanmukham Chetty presents independent India's first Budget | Defence and administration key priorities. Shortest budget period (7.5 months) |
| **1956-58** | T.T. Krishnamachari introduces Wealth Tax | Targeting asset-based inequality |
| **1961** | Income Tax Act, 1961 enacted | Consolidated and codified income tax law |
| **1973** | Y.B. Chavan's "Black Budget" | Record fiscal deficit, oil shock, economic stress |
| **1985-86** | V.P. Singh's reform Budget | Income tax rates reduced, MODVAT introduced, easing of Licence Raj |
| **1991-92** | **Manmohan Singh's "Epochal Budget"** | Economic liberalisation — licensing scrapped, trade opened, foreign investment allowed. Reset India's economic trajectory |
| **1997-98** | **P. Chidambaram's "Dream Budget"** | Major income & corporate tax cuts, voluntary disclosure scheme. Trust-the-taxpayer philosophy |
| **2000** | Yashwant Sinha's "Millennium Budget" | Customs duties on computers lowered, IT/software push, CENVAT introduced |
| **2004-05** | Education Cess introduced | First earmarked tax — taxpayers could see where money goes |
| **2005-06** | VAT introduced | Replaced state-level sales tax |
| **2016-17** | Arun Jaitley merges Rail Budget with Union Budget | Budget date moved to February 1 |
| **2017-07-01** | **GST Rollout** | 17 central & state indirect taxes consolidated. GST Council created. One national market |
| **2020-21** | Nirmala Sitharaman: New Personal Tax Regime | Optional lower-rate structure. Capex-led recovery strategy post-COVID |
| **2023-24** | New Tax Regime made default | Higher rebates, lower surcharge |
| **2025-26** | Zero tax up to ₹12L (new regime) | Major relief for middle class. New IT Bill announced |
| **2026-02-01** | **Union Budget 2026-27** | Total expenditure ₹53.47L Cr. Fiscal deficit 4.3% GDP. Capex ₹12.22L Cr. **New Income Tax Act 2025 effective April 1, 2026.** Interest payments hit ₹14.04L Cr. 50% debt-to-GDP target by 2031. |
