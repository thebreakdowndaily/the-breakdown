## Story 2 — Norway Bans AI in Schools

### X/Twitter

Norway banned smartphones in schools. GPAs went up. Mental health visits went down.

Now they're banning AI for kids under 14.

Tiered system: 6-13 no AI, 14-16 supervised, 17-19 taught AI skills.

The US and UK are debating. Norway is already doing.
thebreakdown.in/story/norway-bans-ai-elementary-schools-june-2026

### LinkedIn

Norway just did what no country has done: nearly ban AI in elementary schools.

Starting August 2026, children aged 6-13 will not be allowed to use AI in school. 14-16 can use it only under teacher supervision.

This is not Luddism. It's data-driven policy — building on Norway's 2024 smartphone ban, which measurably improved GPAs and reduced mental health crises.

The logic is simple: if AI lets children skip the struggle of learning, they never build the foundations.

Many countries ask "how do we integrate AI?" Norway asked a harder question: "should we?"

Their answer — for primary school — is no.
